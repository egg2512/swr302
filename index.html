Save Lives Through Blood Donation

Every drop counts. Join our mission to ensure a stable blood supply for those in need.

Why Donate Blood?

Save Lives

One donation can save up to three lives. Your blood could be the difference between life and death for someone in need.

Easy Process

Our donation process is simple, safe, and takes only about an hour of your time.

Regular Need

Blood is needed every two seconds. Your regular donations help maintain a stable blood supply.

Health Benefits

Donating blood can help reduce the risk of heart disease and stimulate blood cell production.

Understanding Blood Types

A+

Can donate to: A+ and AB+

Can receive from: A+, A-, O+, O-

Key Facts:

  • Second most common blood type
  • Universal platelet donor
  • High demand for plasma

    • B+

    Can donate to: B+ and AB+

    Can receive from: B+, B-, O+, O-

    Key Facts:

  • Third most common blood type
  • Important for B+ and AB+ patients
  • Plasma can help A and AB patients

    • AB+

    Can donate to: AB+ only

    Can receive from: All blood types

    Key Facts:

  • Universal recipient
  • Rarest blood type
  • Universal plasma donor

    • O+

    Can donate to: All positive types

    Can receive from: O+ and O-

    Key Facts:

  • Most common blood type
  • High demand for transfusions
  • Universal red cell donor to positive types

    • A-

    Can donate to: A+, A-, AB+, AB-

    Can receive from: A- and O-

    Key Facts:

  • Rare blood type
  • Important for A- and AB- patients
  • High demand for transfusions

    • B-

    Can donate to: B+, B-, AB+, AB-

    Can receive from: B- and O-

    Key Facts:

  • Very rare blood type
  • Critical for B- and AB- patients
  • Plasma can help B and AB patients

    • AB-

    Can donate to: AB+ and AB-

    Can receive from: All negative types

    Key Facts:

  • Rarest blood type
  • Universal plasma donor
  • Can receive from all negative types

    • O-

    Can donate to: All blood types

    Can receive from: O- only

    Key Facts:

  • Universal donor
  • Most needed blood type
  • Critical for emergency transfusions

    • Important Blood Donation Guidelines

    • You must be at least 17 years old and weigh at least 110 pounds
    • Wait at least 56 days between whole blood donations
    • Bring a valid photo ID when donating
    • Eat a healthy meal and stay hydrated before donating
    • Get plenty of rest the night before donation
    • Avoid strenuous exercise for 24 hours after donation
    • Inform staff of any medications you are taking
    • Wear comfortable clothing with sleeves that can be rolled up

      • ⚠️ Special Note: Negative blood types (especially O-) are critically needed for emergency situations. Your donation could save multiple lives!
